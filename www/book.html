Let's not fight

Right now, at this very moment, pairs of devs everywhere are having heated debates on Native versus Hybrid. For a decision with such large consequences, they may be rightfully so. The argument goes something like this…

“Hybrid apps do not perform like native apps, they are jenky, jittery, slow and so forth. Native code gives you greater flexibility - they are faster, have better local storage and so forth.”

The hybrid evangelist argues back, “you don’t need that much speed and you’re not making a video game for cripes sake, you’re making a simple app. I can have your app done faster and on every platform, including mobile web and web, all at once, all one codebase.”

Ten Pages On Native vs. Hybrid

Some people may be asking why only compare Hybrid versus Native, why not compare HTML5 and Mobile Web as well? I only compare the two because with modern Hybrid frameworks, you get mobile web as a byproduct of the hybrid app. And since HTML5 is used to make hybrid apps, it's a moot point to argue anything other than Hybrid or Native. In my opinion, Hybrid should be cross-platform out of the box - or at least that's how my Hybrid apps work today. Later in this book, I'll share my Hybrid starter projects.

Before we go any further, I have a couple questions for the two devs:

Which one of you can build me my MVP faster? If both answer: in about the same amount of time, 4-5 weeks, they’re probably about right (assuming for the sake of argument, the devs are decent).

Will your users even care or notice your app isn’t native? Is your app more sophisticated than the BBC? What will a native app be able to do that your hybrid app won’t?

Does launching your product cross platform give you important data you’ll need to prove concept? For example, if I show up to a soccer field to promote my soccer app and only 2 out of 10 subscribe and convert because I’m only available on iOS, would saying that 6 out of 10 convert be better?

If you’ve answered my four questions in the following way: same, no and yes, then hybrid has just gained some new popularity. Also, it’s not necessarily an either or scenario. At my last company, we launched hybrid first while we concurrently kicked off native iOS and Android projects on the side. I have friends in opposite situations where they don’t have access to an iOS or Android developer and are doing Hybrid out of necessity.
